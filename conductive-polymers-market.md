<p><p>Conductive polymers refer to a class of materials that can conduct electricity or heat. Electrically conducting polymers have properties similar to metals, allowing them to transmit electrical currents. They find applications in electronic devices, sensors, and batteries. Thermally conducting polymers, on the other hand, have high thermal conductivity and are used in heat management applications such as cooling systems. Both types hold great potential for various industries due to their unique properties and the increasing demand for efficient energy transfer and heat dissipation technologies.</p></p>
