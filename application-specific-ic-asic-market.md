<p><p>Application-Specific Integrated Circuit (ASIC) refers to a type of integrated circuit that is designed for a specific application or task. There are three main market types for ASICs. Full-custom design involves the development of a chip from scratch, allowing for maximum customization but higher development costs and longer timeframes. Standard-cell based ASICs use pre-designed standard cells to create custom circuits, enabling quicker development time and lower costs. Gate-array based ASICs use a prefabricated array of logic gates that can be custom-configured through metal layers, offering faster time-to-market and lower costs compared to full-custom design.</p></p>
