<p><strong>The Emulsion Explosive Sensitizer Market Analysis by types is segmented into:</strong></p>
<p><ul><li>Hollow Glass Microspheres</li><li>Resin Microspheres</li><li>Expanded Perlite</li><li>Sodium Nitrite</li></ul></p>
<p><p>Emulsion explosive sensitizers are substances added to emulsion explosives to enhance their sensitivity and efficiency. There are four types of emulsion explosive sensitizers commonly used - Hollow Glass Microspheres, Resin Microspheres, Expanded Perlite, and Sodium Nitrite. Hollow Glass Microspheres are lightweight, hollow spheres that improve the shock sensitivity of explosives. Resin Microspheres are solid microspheres made of synthetic polymers to increase shock sensitivity and stability. Expanded Perlite is a volcanic glass that enhances the sensitivity and energy release of explosives. Sodium Nitrite is a chemical compound that acts as a sensitizer, improving the detonation velocity and power of emulsion explosives.</p></p>
