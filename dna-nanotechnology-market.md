<p><p>DNA nanotechnology is a rapidly growing field that encompasses various market types. Structural DNA nanotechnology focuses on the design and construction of stable and well-defined DNA nanostructures, allowing for the creation of nanoscale objects with precise shapes and patterns. On the other hand, dynamic DNA nanotechnology involves utilizing DNA molecules as programmable systems that can perform diverse functions, such as sensing, computing, and drug delivery, by utilizing the inherent properties of DNA to self-assemble and reconfigure. Both market types have immense potential in diverse applications, ranging from medicine to material science.</p></p>
