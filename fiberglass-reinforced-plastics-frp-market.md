<p><p>Fiberglass Reinforced Plastics (FRP) refer to a composite material made of a polymer matrix, usually thermosetting or thermoplastic plastics, reinforced with glass fibers. Thermosetting plastic products in the FRP market are materials that undergo irreversible chemical reactions when exposed to heat, resulting in a rigid structure. In contrast, thermoplastic plastic products remain malleable when heated and can be reshaped. Both types have their uses in various industries, including construction, automotive, aerospace, and marine, due to their durability, strength, and resistance to corrosion.</p></p>
