<p><p>A PCI Express (Peripheral Component Interconnect Express) Graphics Card is a hardware device that connects to a computer's motherboard through the PCI Express slot. Factors driving this growth include the increasing demand for high-quality graphics in gaming and entertainment industries, the rapid advancement of virtual reality (VR) and augmented reality (AR) technologies, and the rise in adoption of 4K monitors and displays.</p><p>Additionally, the growing popularity of e-sports and online gaming has led to increased demand for powerful graphics cards to support high-resolution and high-frame-rate gaming experiences.
