<p><p>The Thermal Cyclers market is categorized into various types based on their functionalities. Real-time thermal cyclers, also known as quantitative PCR (qPCR) machines, allow continuous monitoring of the DNA amplification process during the reaction. Gradient thermal cyclers enable the optimization of different annealing temperatures across the sample, making them suitable for experiments with various target sequences. Other types of thermal cyclers encompass traditional machines with basic heating and cooling capabilities. These various market segments cater to different research needs and enable scientists to conduct efficient and accurate DNA amplification and analysis.</p></p>
