<p><p>The orthopedic pins market consists of two main types: non-absorbable and absorbable pins. Non-absorbable pins are made of materials that are not broken down or absorbed by the body. They are typically made of stainless steel or titanium. These pins provide long-term stability and are often used in procedures where a permanent fixation is necessary. On the other hand, absorbable pins are made of materials that can be broken down and absorbed by the body over time. These pins are commonly used for temporary fixation, especially in procedures involving children or smaller bones.</p></p>
