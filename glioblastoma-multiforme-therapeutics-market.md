<p><p>Glioblastoma multiforme (GBM) therapeutics refers to the treatment options available for patients diagnosed with glioblastoma, which is a malignant brain tumor that occurs in the glial cells of the brain or spinal cord. It is the most aggressive type of brain cancer and has a very poor prognosis.</p><p>The treatment for GBM typically involves a combination of surgery, radiation therapy, and chemotherapy. However, due to the aggressive nature of this cancer, recurrence is common, and the overall survival rate remains low. Therefore, continuous research and development efforts are being made to find more effective treatment options.</p><p>The global market for GBM therapeutics has been witnessing growth due to the increasing prevalence of glioblastoma cases, rising healthcare expenditure, and advancements in technology. Additionally, the growing geriatric population and increasing awareness about brain cancer diagnosis and treatment are further contributing to market growth.</p><p>Key trends in the GBM therapeutics market include the development of targeted therapies and immunotherapies. Targeted therapies focus on specific molecules or genes involved in the growth and progression of GBM, allowing for more precise and effective treatment. Immunotherapies, on the other hand, harness the body's immune system to recognize and destroy cancer cells.</p><p>The market is also witnessing a shift towards personalized medicine, where treatment plans are tailored to an individual patient based on their genetic profile and other factors. This approach aims to improve treatment outcomes by providing more precise and targeted therapies.</p><p>Overall, the GBM therapeutics market is expected to grow at a compound annual growth rate (CAGR) of 10.9% during the forecast period. Bevacizumab is a monoclonal antibody that inhibits the formation of blood vessels in tumors, reducing their growth. Carmustine is an alkylating agent that interferes with cancer cell DNA replication.
