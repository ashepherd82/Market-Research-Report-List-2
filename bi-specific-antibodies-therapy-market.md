<p><p>Bi-Specific Antibodies (bsAbs) Therapy refers to a type of medical treatment that uses special antibodies to target multiple disease-causing agents simultaneously. It can be categorized into two market types: Fragment-Based Antibodies and IgG-Based Antibodies. Fragment-Based Antibodies are engineered by combining the binding regions of two different antibodies, allowing them to simultaneously bind to two different targets. IgG-Based Antibodies, on the other hand, are modified versions of the immunoglobulin G antibody that can bind to two different antigens. These market types offer promising solutions for various diseases by enhancing the specificity and potency of antibody therapies.</p></p>
