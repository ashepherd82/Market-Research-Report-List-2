<p><p>High reliability resistors are electronic components used to limit or regulate the flow of electric current in a circuit. Thin film resistors are made by depositing a thin layer of resistive material on a ceramic substrate, providing high precision and stability. Thick film resistors are created by printing a thick layer of resistive paste on a substrate, offering better power handling capabilities and lower cost than thin film resistors. Both types are widely used in various industries such as automotive, aerospace, and telecommunications for their reliability and performance characteristics.</p></p>
