<p><p>Preimplantation Genetic Screening (PGS) is a technique used during in vitro fertilization to screen embryos for genetic abnormalities before implantation. Next-generation sequencing, polymerase chain reaction, fluorescence in situ hybridization, comparative genomic hybridization, and single-nucleotide polymorphism are different types of technologies used in the PGS market. Next-generation sequencing involves analyzing the DNA sequence of the embryo, while polymerase chain reaction amplifies specific DNA regions. Fluorescence in situ hybridization labels specific genes with fluorescent probes, comparative genomic hybridization analyzes DNA copy number variations, and single-nucleotide polymorphism identifies genetic variations at the DNA level.</p></p>
