<p><p>Embedded power generation refers to the decentralized production of electricity through smaller-scale power generation systems that are located close to the point of consumption, such as homes, commercial buildings, or small communities. Embedded power generation offers several advantages, including reduced transmission losses, improved energy efficiency, increased resilience, and reduced dependence on the centralized power grid.</p><p>The embedded power generation market has witnessed significant growth in recent years and is expected to continue growing at a CAGR of 8.8% during the forecast period. The integration of energy storage systems with embedded power generation is another emerging trend, as it allows for the efficient utilization of renewable energy and ensures a reliable power supply during periods of low generation or grid outages.</p><p>Furthermore, the increasing demand for off-grid power solutions in remote or underdeveloped areas is expected to drive market growth. Trigeneration adds the production of cooling or refrigeration along with electricity and heat. Quadgeneration further expands with the addition of other outputs like compressed air, steam, or useful chemicals.
