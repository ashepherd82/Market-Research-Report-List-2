<p><p>Extracorporeal Membrane Oxygenation (ECMO) is a life support technique used for patients with severe heart or lung failure. It provides oxygenation and circulation support by using a machine that pumps blood outside the body, oxygenates it, and returns it to the patient. There are three types of ECMO: Veno-Arterial, which involves blood being drawn from a vein and returned to an artery; Arterio-Venous, where blood is drawn from an artery and returned to a vein; and Veno-Venous, which involves blood being drawn from and returned to veins. These different types are used depending on the specific needs of the patient.</p></p>
