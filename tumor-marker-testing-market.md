<p><p>Tumor marker testing refers to the identification and measurement of specific substances in the body that may indicate the presence of a tumor. There are different types of tumor markers used in this testing. Biochemical markers are substances produced by tumor cells. Oncogenes are specific genes that are associated with tumor formation. Growth factors are proteins that stimulate cell growth and division. Hormones are substances that regulate bodily functions and can be produced by tumors. Colony stimulating factors and lymphokines stimulate the production of blood cells. Immunohistochemical stains help identify specific tumor cells. Other markers include genetic markers and molecular markers.</p></p>
