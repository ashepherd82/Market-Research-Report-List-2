<p><p>DNA sequencing equipment and services can be classified into different market types based on the generation of sequencing technology used. First-generation sequencing refers to the traditional Sanger sequencing method, which identifies DNA bases by utilizing chain termination and gel electrophoresis. Second-generation sequencing involves more advanced techniques like Illumina sequencing, which uses sequencing-by-synthesis and parallel sequencing to obtain millions of DNA sequences simultaneously. Third-generation sequencing includes technologies like PacBio and Nanopore sequencing, which can generate long reads in real-time. Fourth-generation sequencing is a theoretical concept that represents potential future advancements in sequencing methods beyond the current technologies.</p></p>
