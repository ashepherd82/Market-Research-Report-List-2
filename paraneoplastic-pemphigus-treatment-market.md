<p><p>Paraneoplastic pemphigus (PNP) is a rare autoimmune blistering disease that occurs in patients with underlying malignancies. It is characterized by widespread mucocutaneous blistering and can have severe systemic manifestations. The treatment of PNP involves a multi-disciplinary approach and the main goal is to control the underlying malignancy and manage the autoimmune response.</p><p>The primary treatment for PNP involves systemic immunosuppressive therapy to control the autoimmune response. Corticosteroids such as prednisone are commonly used, along with other immunosuppressive drugs like azathioprine, mycophenolate mofetil, and rituximab. Treatment may also involve plasmapheresis or intravenous immunoglobulin therapy to remove circulating autoantibodies.</p><p>In recent years, there have been advancements in the treatment of PNP. Biologics like rituximab, which target specific immune cells, have shown promising results in managing the disease.
