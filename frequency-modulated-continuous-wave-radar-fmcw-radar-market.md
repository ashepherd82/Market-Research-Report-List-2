<p>&nbsp;</p>
<p><p>Frequency-Modulated Continuous-Wave (FMCW) radar is used in various market types. Sawtooth modulation involves linear frequency increment followed by a sudden drop. Triangular modulation gradually increases, then linearly decreases, the frequency. Square-wave modulation instantly jumps to a high frequency and returns to the initial value. Stepped modulation involves a series of frequency steps. Sinusoidal modulation uses a smooth sine wave frequency sweep. Each type has specific use cases in fields like automotive, aerospace, and military for applications like distance measurements, velocity determination, and object detection.</p></p>
