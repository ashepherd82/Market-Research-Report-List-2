<p><p>Cotton processing involves two main market types: lint and cottonseed. Lint refers to the fibrous part of the cotton plant, which is cleaned, spun, and used to make various textile products. The lint market is primarily focused on the production and sale of raw cotton fiber. Cottonseed, on the other hand, is the seed obtained from cotton plants after harvesting the lint. The cottonseed market is centered around processing and utilizing cottonseed oil and meal, which are used in cooking oil, animal feed, and other industrial applications.</p></p>
