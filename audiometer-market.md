<p><p>An audiometer is a device used to measure the hearing abilities of an individual. It is commonly used in audiology clinics and hospitals to diagnose hearing disorders and provide appropriate treatment. Audiometers usually consist of a set of headphones or earphones, a microphone, and a control unit. The device generates pure tones or tones with different frequencies and intensities which are played to the patient. The patient then responds to the presented sounds, allowing the audiologist to determine their hearing thresholds.</p><p>The current outlook of the audiometer market is promising, with a growing demand for hearing diagnostic devices due to the increasing prevalence of hearing loss globally.
