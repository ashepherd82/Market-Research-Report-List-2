<p><p>Distribution transformers are electrical devices used to transfer electrical energy between two or more electrical circuits via electromagnetic induction. They are categorized into single-phase and three-phase transformers based on the number of windings present in the transformer core. Single-phase transformers have two windings, whereas three-phase transformers have three windings. Single-phase transformers are commonly used for residential applications, while three-phase transformers are predominantly used in industrial and commercial settings due to their ability to handle higher power loads.</p></p>
