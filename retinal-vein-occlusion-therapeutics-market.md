<p><p>Retinal Vein Occlusion (RVO) is a condition where the blood flow in the veins of the retina is partially or completely blocked, leading to vision loss. There are two main types of RVO: Branch Retinal Vein Occlusion (BRVO) and Central Retinal Vein Occlusion (CRVO). BRVO occurs when a branch of the retinal vein is blocked, while CRVO happens when the main retinal vein is blocked. The therapeutics market for RVO aims to develop treatments and medications that can improve blood flow, reduce swelling, and prevent further damage to the retina, thus improving vision for patients with BRVO or CRVO.</p></p>
