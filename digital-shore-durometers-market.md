<p><p>Digital shore durometers are devices used to measure the hardness of various materials. They come in different types, including Shore A, Shore B, Shore C, Shore D, Shore M, and Shore O. Shore A is suitable for soft materials like rubber, while Shore B is ideal for rigid foam. Shore C is used for materials with intermediate hardness, such as thermoplastics. Shore D is designed for hard materials like plastic, while Shore M is for measuring the hardness of materials like viscoelastic. Lastly, Shore O is used for measuring the hardness of soft elastomers and gel-like materials.</p></p>
