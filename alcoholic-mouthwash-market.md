<p><p>Alcoholic mouthwash can be classified into two market types: therapeutic mouthwash and cosmetic mouthwash. Therapeutic mouthwash is specifically formulated to provide oral health benefits such as reducing plaque, preventing gum diseases, and freshening breath. It usually contains active ingredients like fluoride, antimicrobial agents, and essential oils. On the other hand, cosmetic mouthwash is primarily focused on providing temporary relief from bad breath and improving the aesthetic appeal of the mouth. It may contain ingredients like alcohol, flavoring agents, and breath fresheners without any therapeutic properties.</p></p>
