<p><p>Dental air filtration refers to the process of removing harmful particles, contaminants, and pathogens from the air in dental offices and clinics. There are two main types of air filtration systems commonly used in this industry: ULPA (Ultra-Low Penetration Air) filtration and HEPA (High-Efficiency Particulate Air) filtration. ULPA filtration is highly effective in removing very small particles, including bacteria and viruses, ensuring clean and safe air. On the other hand, HEPA filtration removes larger particles and is widely used to purify the air in dental settings, providing a healthier environment for both patients and dental professionals.</p></p>
